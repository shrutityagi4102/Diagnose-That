About Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a potentially serious infectious disease that mainly affects the lungs. The bacteria that cause tuberculosis are spread from person to person through tiny droplets released into the air via coughs and sneezes.

Early Infection

This is when the host first comes into contact with TB bacteria. If the body is strong enough, it will fight off the TB bacteria and not become infected.

Secondary TB

When someone with Latent TB: Goes through mental, physical or emotional stress Grows older Contracts a serious disease

Active TB

Tuberculosis germs can start transmitting. The tuberculosis bacteria can also travel from the lungs to other parts of the body by travelling through the bloodstream
